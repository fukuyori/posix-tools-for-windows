Dear Rachel Reeves,

When you stood up in November to announce your Budget, you told pub landlords like myself that we would see the lowest tax rates since 1991. But it is now clear that my business rates bill will jump from next-to-nothing to £800 in April.

You have more than doubled the “rateable value” of my pub – the measure of how much it is worth, which is used to calculate business rates. Even though you have lowered the multiplier applied to this, our tax bill is a whopping £712 more per month, despite a fancy headline stating business rates are dropping. It feels very sneaky...
